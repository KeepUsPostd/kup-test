1. Information We Collect

Information You Provide to Us

When you create an account, complete your profile, or use KeepUsPostd's features, you provide us with information that we collect and store. This includes:

  • Account Information: Your name, email address, phone number, date of birth, username, password, and profile photo.
  • Profile Information: Biography, location, social media handles, areas of interest, and any other details you choose to add to your profile.
  • Content and Communications: Posts, photos, videos, comments, messages, reviews, and any other content you create or share through the platform, as well as metadata associated with that content (such as the date, time, and location of creation).
  • Transaction Information: When you participate in campaigns, receive rewards, or make payments through KeepUsPostd, we collect details about those transactions, including reward amounts, payment methods, and payout history.
  • Support Communications: If you contact our support team, we collect the content of your messages, your email address, and any other information you choose to provide.

Information from Your Networks and Connections

When you connect third-party social media accounts (such as Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Facebook, or X/Twitter) to KeepUsPostd, we may collect information from those platforms in accordance with your privacy settings on those services. This may include:

  • Your public profile information (name, handle, bio, profile image).
  • Follower counts, engagement metrics, and audience demographics.
  • Content you have posted publicly on those platforms.
  • Contact lists or connections, only if you explicitly grant us permission.

Information Collected Automatically

When you access or use KeepUsPostd, we automatically collect certain information about your device and usage patterns, including:

  • Device Information: Hardware model, operating system and version, unique device identifiers, mobile network information, and browser type.
  • Usage Data: Pages and features you access, actions you take within the platform, time spent on pages, search queries, referring URLs, and crash or error reports.
  • Location Data: Approximate location derived from your IP address. If you grant permission, we may also collect precise location data from your mobile device to enable location-based features.
  • Log Data: Server logs that record your IP address, access times, pages viewed, and other system activity.

Information from Third-Party Partners

We may receive information about you from third-party partners, including:

  • Analytics providers who help us understand platform usage.
  • Advertising partners who provide information about your interactions with advertisements.
  • Business partners who collaborate with us to offer co-branded services or campaigns.
  • Publicly available sources such as public social media profiles and business registrations.

4. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website or use an application. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, provide reporting information, and assist with personalization.

How We Use Cookies

KeepUsPostd and our third-party partners use cookies and similar tracking technologies (such as web beacons, pixels, and local storage) for the following purposes:

  • Essential Cookies: These are necessary for the platform to function properly. They enable core features such as authentication, security, and session management. You cannot opt out of essential cookies.
  • Performance and Analytics Cookies: These cookies collect information about how you use the platform, such as which pages you visit and whether you encounter errors. This data helps us improve the platform and understand user behavior. All information collected by these cookies is aggregated and anonymous.
  • Functionality Cookies: These cookies remember your preferences and settings (such as language and region) to provide a more personalized experience.
  • Advertising and Targeting Cookies: These cookies are used to deliver advertisements that are relevant to your interests. They may also limit the number of times you see an advertisement and help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.

Session vs. Persistent Cookies

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser. They are used to maintain your session while you navigate the platform.
  • Persistent Cookies: These cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. They are used to remember your preferences and login status across visits.

First-Party vs. Third-Party Cookies

  • First-Party Cookies: Set by KeepUsPostd directly when you visit our platform.
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by our partners and service providers (such as analytics and advertising providers) when you use our platform. These third parties have their own privacy policies governing the use of these cookies.

How to Control Cookies

You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies, though doing so may affect the functionality of the platform. You can also opt out of interest-based advertising by visiting the Digital Advertising Alliance at www.aboutads.info/choices or the Network Advertising Initiative at www.networkadvertising.org/choices.

On mobile devices, you can limit ad tracking through your device settings (for example, "Limit Ad Tracking" on iOS or "Opt Out of Ads Personalization" on Android).

5. Your Privacy Rights New

Depending on where you reside, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information under applicable privacy laws. KeepUsPostd is committed to honoring these rights regardless of your location, to the extent reasonably feasible.

California Residents (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

  • Right to Know: You have the right to request that we disclose the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the categories of sources from which we collected it, the business or commercial purpose for collecting it, and the categories of third parties with whom we share it.
  • Right to Delete: You have the right to request that we delete the personal information we have collected about you, subject to certain exceptions provided by law (such as when the information is needed to complete a transaction, comply with a legal obligation, or detect security incidents).
  • Right to Correct: You have the right to request that we correct inaccurate personal information that we maintain about you.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale or Sharing: You have the right to opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of your personal information. KeepUsPostd does not sell your personal information. We do not exchange personal data for monetary consideration. To the extent any sharing of data with third parties constitutes a "sale" or "share" under the CCPA, you may opt out by contacting us at the address below.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your privacy rights. You will not receive different pricing, quality, or levels of service for making a privacy request.

Other State Privacy Laws

Residents of Virginia (VCDPA), Colorado (CPA), Connecticut (CTDPA), Utah (UCPA), Texas (TDPSA), Oregon (OCPA), Montana (MCDPA), and other states with applicable privacy legislation may have similar rights, including:

  • The right to access the personal data we hold about you.
  • The right to delete your personal data.
  • The right to correct inaccurate personal data.
  • The right to opt out of targeted advertising, sale of personal data, and profiling in furtherance of decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects.
  • The right to data portability (receiving your data in a portable, readily usable format).

How to Exercise Your Rights

To submit a privacy rights request, you may:

  • Email us at privacy@keepuspostd.com.
  • Write to us at the mailing address provided in Section 11 of this policy.

We will verify your identity before processing your request. You may be asked to provide information that matches our records to confirm your identity. We will respond to verifiable requests within 45 days, or within the timeframe required by applicable law. If we need additional time, we will notify you of the extension and the reason for it.

Authorized Agents

You may designate an authorized agent to submit a privacy rights request on your behalf. The authorized agent must provide proof of authorization (such as a signed written authorization or a power of attorney). We may contact you directly to verify the request and confirm your identity.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. The specific retention periods are as follows:

  • Account Data: Retained for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account, we will delete or de-identify your account data within 30 days, except where retention is required for legal, regulatory, or legitimate business purposes.
  • Transaction Data: Retained for 7 years after the transaction date to comply with tax, financial reporting, and legal obligations.
  • Usage Logs: Retained for 12 months from the date of collection, after which they are aggregated or deleted.
  • Search History: Deleted after 6 months from the date of the search.
  • Backup Copies: Backup copies of your data may persist for up to 90 days after deletion from our primary systems before being permanently removed.
  • Legal Holds: If your data is subject to a legal hold, litigation, regulatory investigation, or other legal obligation, we may retain it for longer than the periods stated above, as required by law.

When retention is no longer necessary, we will securely delete or de-identify your information so that it can no longer be associated with you.
